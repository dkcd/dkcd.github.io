Butter Chicken

Delicious North Indian Main Course Dish

Best accompanied with chawal/pulao (rice) or naan/paraatha (breads)

  • Serving size: 4 people
  • Preparation time: 1 hour
  • Difficulty level: Easy

Ingredients:

  1. Chicken breast - 500 gm
  2. Yogurt (preferably Greek) - 1 cup (150 gm)
  3. Tomato puree - 200 gm
  4. Thick cream (30%) - 200 gm
  5. Butter (salted) - 2 tablespoons
  6. Onions - 2 big ones
  7. Garlic - 2 cloves
  8. Olive oil - 5 tablespoons
  9. Chicken masala (powdered spice) - 2 teaspoon
  10. Salt - As required
  11. Black pepper - As required
  12. Red chili powder - As required
  13. Water - As required

Procedure:

Part 1 - Marinating the chicken (45 minutes)
  1. Cut the chicken in bite-sized chunks.
  2. Dump them in a bowl, add 1 tablespoon of olive oil, all the yogurt, ½ teaspoon of chicken masala, salt, pepper, and red chili as per your taste and spiciness level.
  3. Mix them well with your hands and make sure all pieces are coated with the mixture evenly.
  4. Keep this bowl in fridge for 45 minutes, this will help the chicken absorb the paste properly, giving it a deep taste.
Part 2 - Preparing the gravy (10 minutes)
  1. Cut the onion as well as garlic in big chunks.
  2. Add them to a mixer/grinder and grind them into a fine paste/pulp.
  3. Heat the olive oil on medium heat in a big, deep pan. Add the onion paste to it as soon as it gets heated enough.
  4. Heat the paste till it gets a light brown hue, don’t let it get black.
  5. Now add the tomato puree, cream, and butter. Start stirring till it becomes a consistent creamy red. Add water to change the thickness of the gravy as per your wish. I advise to make it not too watery, keep it thick.
  6. Now add 1½ teaspoon chicken masala, salt, pepper, and red chili as per your taste and spiciness level. Mix well. Let it heat up before adding the chicken.
Part 3 - Final cooking (10 minutes)
  1. Now add the marinated chicken to the gravy and spread it out so the pieces don’t stick.
  2. Keep the flame to low and cover it with a lid for 10 minutes. Take care, the gravy might bubble and splatter around!
  3. After 10 minutes cut a chicken piece to test if it is raw or not. If all is well then take it off the stove and serve hot!!!
